Amazon

Here's the timeline of Amazon

  • 1994: Amazon was founded by Jeff Bezos in Seattle, Washington, USA. It initially started as an online bookstore.
  • 1995: Amazon began selling books online and later expanded to sell various products.
  • 1997: Amazon went public and became a publicly traded company on NASDAQ.
  • 2000: Amazon introduced its personalized recommendation system known as the "Recommendation Engine."
  • 2005: Amazon launched the Prime membership program, offering additional benefits to customers.
  • 2007: Amazon released its first e-reader, the Kindle.
  • 2014: Amazon introduced its AI assistant, Amazon Echo.
  • 2017: Amazon entered the grocery market by acquiring the traditional retailer Whole Foods Market.
  • 2020: Amazon emphasized the importance of its online services and logistics network during the COVID-19 pandemic, with a surge in online sales.
  • 2021: Jeff Bezos transitioned from CEO to Executive Chairman, with Andy Jassy taking over as CEO. Amazon also expanded its online streaming service, Amazon Prime Video.
